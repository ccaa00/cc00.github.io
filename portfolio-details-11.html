Disaster Drills in Taiwan

Overview

Natural disasters cannot be avoided. Only by strengthening the knowledge of disaster prevention, taking precautions before disasters, and improving self-disaster resilience, can reduce disaster losses and ensure the safety of lives and properties. To improve national disaster prevention awareness and national disaster prevention and response capacity, and promote evacuation proficiency, we provided a complete process from disaster reporting, resource dispatching, shelter management to losses survey.

We collaborated with local governments and communities many times. Through our services, rescue teams could report disaster situations with words, photos, and geolocation to the command center. Then, the command center could allocate resources by integrating all conditions reported, and assigned missions to the people or teams in charge directly on the platform, and those who were assigned could get notified immediately. In shelter management, staff could register refugees on the platform by scanning or entering identity numbers with the application on cell phones, and distribute supplies to refugees and oversee the supplies inventory in real time. After the disasters, the surveyors could use the application to check the damage and losses with words, pictures, and geolocation.
