Training Courses and Mapping Activities in Taiwan

Overview

Disasters always happen unexpectedly, hence it is important to prepare in normal times. The first thing is to establish a detailed and up-to-date geographic database to be a reference direction for disaster response planning and resource allocation. We presented a mapping application that could easily create and change the information of buildings and roads on OpenStreetMap (OSM). OpenStreetMap is one of the most popular free and open-source maps with over 2 million registered users all over the globe. It aims to create a free editable geographic database of the world, and the geodata underlying the maps is considered the primary output of OpenStreetMap.

Our application allowed users to create and edit objects in OpenStreetMap. It supported creating nodes, lines, and polygons on the map, and editing points of interest features like building types, materials, entrances, levels, and so on. To enhance the resilience of disasters, we planned to complete the regional maps, and turned the data we collected into an evacuation map. We proposed our plan to local communities and governments, and got approval. Then, we designed courses for residents using the mapping application. After that, we arranged mapping activities to refine existing map data. Finally, all the data was presented and discussed by locals to build an evacuation map.
