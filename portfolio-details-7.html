Training Courses, Mapping Activities, and Disaster Drills in South East Asia

Overview

The best way to strengthen the resilience from disasters is to institute a series of preparations, from collecting map data, developing evacuation maps to drills. We established an information-based Disaster Risk Management through different platforms in 4 pilot countries, Armenia, Bangladesh, Philippines, and Fiji. Utilized our providing integrated solutions, four countries in South Asia progressed their system of disaster response abilities.

The first thing was to improve the earlier map database. We had them attend the tool training courses to learn how to use our mapping and reporting tools. The next was conducting mapping activities. We walked around in towns refining the town map with volunteers and used the data we collected to create evacuation maps. Lastly, we made drill plans with local governments and communities and executed them. During drills, the residents evacuated according to the evacuation maps, and the rescue teams ran rescue activities and reported the damage and losses back to the command center. Based on this return information, the command center would be able to distribute resources effectively to reduce disaster losses and improve disaster relief efficiency.
