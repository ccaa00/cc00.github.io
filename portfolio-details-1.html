Applying Space-Based Technology and Information and Communication Technology to Strengthen Disaster Resilience

Overview

This technical assistance (TA) project aims to establish an information-based disaster risk management through the application of Space-Based Technology (SBT) and Information and Communication Technology (ICT) in 4 pilot countries, Armenia, Bangladesh, Philippines, and Fiji.

We established an information platform where the rescue teams could report disasters with photos and geolocation back to the command center, and the command center could leverage this information to distribute resources in order to make the relief operation more efficient. Also, the local governments could use it for surveying the loss of disasters, and set up the database for enhancing disaster resilience. Moreover, to response to the places with poor internet signal and without internet signal, such as some rural areas and the places that disaster destroyed infrastructures. Local governments could establish their own local servers. The surveyors and rescue teams could use the application offline service or Short Message Service (SMS) to report disasters, and when the application would send the reports immediately as the signal got back or they went back to the command center.
